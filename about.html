About Fruits

The Science Behind Fruits

What Makes a Fruit a Fruit?

Botanically speaking, a fruit is the mature ovary of a flowering plant, typically containing seeds. This means that tomatoes, cucumbers, and even nuts are technically fruits!

Ripening Process

Many fruits produce ethylene gas as they ripen, which triggers the ripening process in other fruits nearby. This is why placing a ripe banana with unripe fruits can speed up their ripening.

Color and Nutrition

The vibrant colors in fruits come from various compounds like anthocyanins (purple/red), carotenoids (orange/yellow), and chlorophyll (green). These compounds often have antioxidant properties.

Fruit Categories

Citrus Fruits

Oranges, lemons, limes, and grapefruits are rich in vitamin C and citric acid, giving them their characteristic tangy taste.

Stone Fruits

Peaches, plums, cherries, and apricots have a hard pit (stone) in the center containing the seed.

Berries

True berries like grapes and blueberries have seeds embedded in the flesh, while strawberries are actually not berries at all!
